# Stakeholders

This document defines all stakeholder roles that interact with the SRS Review Agent — who they are, what they are responsible for, how they receive findings, and what they do with them.

Every role listed here either generates inputs to the agent, receives findings from it, or both. Understanding these roles is a prerequisite for configuring the agent's routing rules and for any team planning a deployment.

---

## How findings reach stakeholders

The agent routes findings based on two factors: the type of change detected (clinical, risk/safety, UI/workflow, security, AI/ML, non-functional) and the document ownership configuration in the agent's product registry. A finding is routed to the document owner responsible for the controlled document most affected by the detected gap.

Every finding shows the receiving stakeholder:
- What changed or what gap was detected
- Which standard clause was violated or at risk
- The IMDRF category and software item safety class that determined the finding's severity
- The recommended action
- The disposition options available

All findings are non-binding. The stakeholder reviews the finding, records a disposition with a rationale, and takes whatever action they judge appropriate. The agent logs the disposition but does not enforce any outcome.

---

## Disposition states

Every finding must reach a closed state. The available dispositions are:

| Disposition | Meaning | Required input |
|---|---|---|
| **Accept** | The finding is valid and action will be taken | Rationale (one sentence minimum) |
| **Reject** | The finding is not applicable in this context | Rationale explaining why (mandatory) |
| **Defer** | Valid but will be addressed in a later cycle | Target date or milestone |
| **Clarify** | The finding needs more information before a decision | Question or clarification request |
| **Route** | This finding belongs to a different owner | Target role or person |
| **Escalate** | This finding requires attention above the normal review level | Escalation reason |
| **Out of scope** | The finding references something outside the current review boundary | Brief explanation |

All dispositions are logged with: finding ID, disposition state, reviewer identity, timestamp, and rationale text. This log is immutable once written and constitutes the agent's audit trail.

---

## Finding severity levels

Severity is determined by the combination of IMDRF significance category and IEC 62304 software item safety class. See `docs/00-overview/classification-reference.md` for the full calibration matrix. The following levels apply across all use cases:

| Severity | Definition | Required action | Owner |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Critical** | Regulatory non-compliance, missing safety-critical requirement, mandatory escalation condition | Must be dispositioned before the next controlled document approval | Assigned owner + RA/PRRC notification |
| **High** | Significant traceability gap, untestable requirement, high-probability deficiency risk | Should be dispositioned before the next design phase milestone | Assigned owner |
| **Medium** | Minor gap, clarity issue, potential traceability weakness | Address in current review cycle or defer with documented rationale | Assigned owner |
| **Low** | Informational, best practice recommendation, no immediate compliance risk | Consider for future improvement | Team discretion |

## Stakeholder roles

---

### Requirements Engineer / Systems Engineer

**What they own:** The SRS. They are the primary author and maintainer of software requirements.

**How they interact with the agent:** They receive findings on every change event that touches the SRS. They are the first reviewer for most findings before escalation to specialist roles. They also receive SRS suggestions generated by UC2 (baseline-to-SRS mapping) when a new baseline document is approved.

**Responsibilities:**
- Review agent findings before finalising or approving a requirement
- Address identified gaps — ambiguity, missing traceability, missing acceptance criteria, missing safety classification
- Maintain stable requirement identifiers across SRS revisions — modified, split, merged, or deprecated requirements must retain traceable IDs
- Document disposition rationale for every finding, including rejections
- Re-trigger analysis after significant SRS updates to confirm gaps are resolved

**What to do with findings by type:**
- *Missing risk control link* — verify with the Risk Engineer whether the SRS requirement implementing that control exists and is correctly identified. Accept or reject based on that verification.
- *Untestable requirement* — rewrite the requirement to include measurable acceptance criteria before accepting the finding.
- *Missing safety classification* — confirm the software item safety class with the Software Architect, then add the classification tag to the requirement.
- *Reclassification flag* — do not disposition independently. Route immediately to the Software Architect and Risk Engineer for joint assessment.

---

### Software Architect

**What they own:** The Software Architecture Document (SAD). They define the decomposition of the software system into items and assign IEC 62304 safety classes to each item.

**How they interact with the agent:** They receive findings related to architectural feasibility, software item safety class, and reclassification triggers. They are the authoritative source for the software item registry that the agent uses for classification calibration.

**Responsibilities:**
- Maintain the software item registry in the agent's configuration — item identifiers, safety classes, and isolation arguments
- Review findings that flag potential safety class reclassification — a SRS change that removes or weakens an isolation argument requires architectural reassessment
- Confirm that security requirements flagged by the agent are architecturally sound and implementable
- Provide feasibility feedback on requirements that cannot be implemented as specified

**What to do with findings by type:**
- *Potential safety class reclassification* — assess whether the SRS change actually affects the isolation argument. If it does, update the SAD and the software item registry before the SRS is approved. Document the outcome in the finding disposition.
- *Architectural feasibility concern* — work with the Requirements Engineer to rewrite the requirement before accepting.

---

### Risk / Safety Engineer

**What they own:** The Risk Management File — the full ISO 14971 chain from hazards through risk controls to verification evidence.

**How they interact with the agent:** They receive findings from UC3 (risk traceability check) whenever a SRS change affects safety-relevant requirements. They are the authoritative source for the risk control traceability table that UC3 uses.

**Responsibilities:**
- Maintain the risk control traceability table — risk control ID to SRS requirement ID mapping — as a controlled document
- Review findings that identify missing or broken risk control links
- Update the risk management file when SRS changes introduce new hazardous situations or modify existing risk controls
- Jointly assess reclassification findings with the Software Architect
- Verify that formative usability study use errors that map to existing hazards have been assessed in the risk file (UC7)

**What to do with findings by type:**
- *Missing risk control link* — verify whether the gap is real (the SRS requirement implementing the control does not exist) or a metadata issue (the requirement exists but is not linked). Fix the root cause — either add the requirement or fix the traceability link.
- *Reclassification flag* — assess jointly with the Software Architect. If the item's safety class must increase, update the risk management file to reflect the new risk profile of the affected item.
- *Post-summative SRS change affecting safety* — assess whether the change invalidates any summative study risk acceptance. Escalate to RA/PRRC if it does.

---

### Regulatory Affairs Specialist / PRRC

**What they own:** Regulatory strategy, submission management, and overall technical file or DHF integrity. In EU-regulated organisations, the PRRC (Person Responsible for Regulatory Compliance, EU MDR Article 15) has a mandatory accountability role.

**How they interact with the agent:** They receive escalated findings — Critical severity, reclassification flags, and findings that may affect submission status. They are the routing target for any finding that has potential market impact.

**Responsibilities:**
- Review and disposition Critical findings before SRS approval milestones
- Assess whether a detected gap or reclassification trigger affects the current submission or requires a new submission
- Review disposition rationales for accepted and rejected findings as part of audit preparation
- Ensure the agent's audit log is exported and integrated into the technical file or DHF at appropriate intervals
- For EU organisations: confirm that findings involving AI Act obligations (Article 14, Article 83) are assessed in addition to MDR obligations

**What to do with findings by type:**
- *Potential significant change* — assess whether the detected gap, if unresolved, would constitute a significant change requiring a new submission. Document the assessment outcome.
- *Reclassification flag at device level* — initiate a formal classification reassessment. Do not allow the SRS to be approved while the reassessment is in progress.
- *AI Act Article 83 boundary concern* — assess whether the proposed SRS change stays within the approved change control plan. If it does not, a new conformity assessment is required before the change is implemented.

---

### Quality Assurance / Validation Engineer

**What they own:** Verification and validation plans, test specifications, and test evidence.

**How they interact with the agent:** They receive findings related to testability gaps — requirements that lack measurable acceptance criteria, requirements without corresponding test cases, and changes that may invalidate existing test coverage.

**Responsibilities:**
- Review changes between SRS versions to assess impact on existing test cases and verification plans
- Flag requirements that cannot be adequately tested and return them to the Requirements Engineer
- Ensure each accepted finding that results in a SRS update is reflected in the corresponding test plan
- Maintain traceability from SRS requirements through to test case coverage

**What to do with findings by type:**
- *Untestable requirement* — confirm with the Requirements Engineer that the requirement has been rewritten with measurable acceptance criteria before accepting the finding.
- *Test coverage gap* — create the missing test case and update the traceability matrix before marking the finding as resolved.

---

### Cybersecurity Engineer

**What they own:** The cybersecurity documentation — threat model, SBOM, vulnerability tracking records, and security risk assessments.

**How they interact with the agent:** They receive findings from UC5 (cybersecurity change impact) when SRS changes introduce or modify interfaces, data flows, authentication, or encryption requirements.

**Responsibilities:**
- Maintain the threat model in a structured, machine-readable format for the agent to query
- Review security findings against the current threat model
- Update the threat model when new interfaces or data flows are introduced
- Manage the SBOM and assess vulnerability disclosures for SOUP components that may require SRS updates
- Confirm that security requirements meet IEC 81001-5-1 and OWASP ASVS obligations

**What to do with findings by type:**
- *Missing security requirement* — verify against the threat model whether the new interface or data flow introduces a threat that is not yet mitigated. If it does, add the security requirement to the SRS and update the threat model.
- *SOUP vulnerability* — assess whether the vulnerability affects a component that implements an SRS requirement. If it does, initiate a change to the affected requirement or add a mitigation requirement.

---

### Usability / Human Factors Specialist

**What they own:** The usability engineering file — formative and summative study plans, use error analysis, and usability risk assessments.

**How they interact with the agent:** They receive findings from UC7 (usability engineering lifecycle integration) when SRS changes affect user interface behaviour or when formative study results require SRS updates.

**Responsibilities:**
- Submit completed formative study results to the agent for use error analysis and SRS impact assessment
- Review post-summative SRS change flags — any SRS change after a summative study baseline must be assessed for its impact on study validity
- Confirm that use errors identified in formative studies that map to existing hazards have been communicated to the Risk Engineer
- Note: IEC 62366-1 obligations apply at the system level. Software item safety class decomposition does not reduce the scope of the summative study.

**What to do with findings by type:**
- *Formative use error requiring SRS update* — work with the Requirements Engineer to add or modify the relevant SRS requirements. Confirm with the Risk Engineer that the use error has been assessed against the risk file.
- *Post-summative SRS change flag* — assess whether the change affects any use scenario that was evaluated in the summative study. If it does, determine whether a supplemental study section or new study is required. Escalate to RA/PRRC if a new study is needed before the next submission.

---

### Clinical Affairs Specialist

**What they own:** Clinical evaluation documentation — Clinical Evaluation Plan (CEP) and Clinical Evaluation Report (CER) in the EU; clinical evidence package for FDA submissions.

**How they interact with the agent:** They receive findings when SRS changes affect clinical performance claims, intended population, or clinical workflow constraints.

**Responsibilities:**
- Review findings that flag a potential change to clinical claims or performance specifications
- Assess whether a SRS change that modifies clinical performance requirements necessitates an update to the clinical evaluation
- For AI-enabled products: assess whether a SRS change to algorithm performance specifications affects the clinical validity argument

**What to do with findings by type:**
- *Clinical claim change* — assess whether the modified SRS requirement constitutes a change to the clinical claim. If it does, initiate a clinical evaluation update before the next submission.

---

### Quality Manager / Management Representative

**What they own:** The QMS — quality system procedures, CAPA management, and audit management.

**How they interact with the agent:** They use the agent's audit log export for inspection preparation and QMS evidence. They receive escalated findings that require management attention.

**Responsibilities:**
- Ensure the agent's audit log is incorporated into the QMS audit trail at appropriate intervals
- Review trends in finding types and disposition rates as quality indicators
- Manage escalations that have not been resolved within the agreed review cycle time

---

## Roles not covered by the agent

The following roles interact with the controlled documents the agent monitors but do not interact directly with the agent:

**Clinical users / intended users** — the end users of the SaMD product. Their needs are captured in user needs documents and usability studies, which feed into the SRS. They do not directly interact with the agent.

**Manufacturing and service personnel** — responsible for device production and post-market service. Their requirements may appear in the SRS as maintenance or serviceability requirements but they are not routing targets for agent findings.

**Notified body / FDA reviewers** — regulatory authority reviewers assess the technical file or DHF during conformity assessment or premarket review. They do not interact with the agent directly. The agent's audit log export is the primary evidence artefact for demonstrating systematic change control to these reviewers.
